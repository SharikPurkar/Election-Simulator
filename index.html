Premise

In the 2016 Presidential Election more than 6 million Americans were ineligble for voting as a result of draconian voter disenfranchisment laws at the state level. Only Maine and Vermont allow all individuals in the criminal justice system to vote regardless of their legal status, the simulation below shows that the election may have turned if all states adopted similar policies. Of these disenfranchising states, in eleven of them the margin of victory for the winning candidate was smaller than the margin of disenfranchised individuals in the given state, eight and three of these states went for Trump and Clinton respectively during the election but may have had swayed outcomes had voter disenfranchisment laws been different. Additionally the 2016 election brought upon another rarely seen phenomenon, one candidate winning the popular while loosing the electoral college. The simulator below shows the victor of the 2016 election had it been computed three different ways; The current winner take all system, splitting electoral votes at the state level, and a national popular vote. On the grid below the Y and X Axis represent voter turnout and voter lean respectively.
